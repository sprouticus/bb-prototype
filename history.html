Then to Now

Three Vehicles, One Idea.

Off-road, work-capable, and built around the rider — from the first Buddy Buggy to what's coming next.

~2016

Where It Started

The Buddy Buggy

About a decade ago, Dave White built the Buddy Buggy — the first wheelchair-adaptive off-road vehicle out of the shop. Board through a rear wheelchair lift, dock straight into the driver's seat, and steer with a joystick. No transfers, no compromises, all-electric. Built and based in Albuquerque, NM, the Buddy Buggy went wherever people needed it: hunting trips, farms and ranches, and just getting back outside. It proved the idea and set up everything that came after it.

~2020

The Next Generation

The Enabler 2.0

Everything learned building the Buddy Buggy went into its successor. Starting around 2020, the vehicle was rebuilt from the frame up: all-wheel drive, an all-electric powertrain, a self-loading rear wheelchair lift the driver operates alone, and all-steel construction rated for real work, not just recreation. It's the vehicle you see across this site today, and it's still in production — see the full walkthrough or the complete specs.
