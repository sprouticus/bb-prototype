Our Story

Built in New Mexico. Built for Independence.

Two people, a workshop, and a problem worth solving.

How we got here

B.B. Engineering started with Dave and Grace White, working out of New Mexico. Dave spent decades in sales and traditional marketing before deciding to build something himself — a vehicle that didn't ask wheelchair users to settle for less capability than anyone else on the job site or the trail.

The result is the Enabler 2.0: an all-electric, off-road utility vehicle with a self-loading wheelchair lift, designed and produced in the U.S.A. It's a small company right now. That's by design — we'd rather build something that works and grow with the people who buy it than rush a product we haven't proven out.

Origin

A vehicle built around the rider

Started as a question: why should an off-road, work-capable vehicle be out of reach for wheelchair users? The Enabler 2.0 was engineered from the frame up to answer that.

Today

Early production, real interest

We're taking waitlist signups from individuals and early conversations with organizations who want the Enabler 2.0 on their grounds and campuses.

Ahead

Growing with our customers

As volume picks up, so does our capacity — more units, more support, and a closer relationship with the people and organizations putting these vehicles to work.

Why We Build for Work, Not Just Recreation

Outdoor work shouldn't be off-limits.

We started by building a vehicle for personal freedom. Now we're working with campuses, parks departments, municipalities, and facilities crews who want to put mobility-impaired staff to work outdoors — grounds, maintenance, patrol — instead of routing them to a desk by default.

1.5 million more disabled workers have entered the U.S. workforce since 2020, but only 3 to 4 percent receive any kind of equipment-based accommodation. The Enabler 2.0 is built to be part of closing that gap.
